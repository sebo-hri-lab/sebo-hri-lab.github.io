For New Lab Members

Getting Involved with the Lab as a UChicago Undergraduate

Getting Involved. The best way to get involved with the lab is by joining an ongoing research project. Research project work may entail doing literature reviews of related work to inform our intended research projects, programming robots to interact with people, designing and testing questionnaires to administer to human participants, recruiting and running human participants in our designed experiments, and analysis of data that we collect from our experiments. Once you have gained sufficient experience helping out with some research projects, you are welcome to propose your own new research project that youâ€™d like to lead.

Joining a Research Project. To join a research project, we recommend talking with some of the students in the lab meeting to find out what they're working on right now and if their project has room for you to join. Additionally, you can talk with Sarah to get a broader overview of the projects going on in the lab and which ones might have openings.


Get Connected

The best ways to get connected with the HRI lab are to:

  1. Join the HRI Lab Slack: https://sebolab.slack.com (anyone with a @uchicago.edu email address can join).
  2. Attend our weekly lab meetings.
  3. Start helping out with an ongoing project in the lab -- please talk with current members and Sarah to determine what project might be a good fit for you to join.


Get Access to the HRI Lab (JCl 379)

Please send a Slack DM or email Sarah (Sarah, sarahsebo@uchicago.edu) so I can add you to the access list for the lab. Once you're added, you'll have 24/7 access to the lab.


Add Yourself to the HRI Lab Website

We'd love to add you to the People page of our website. Please follow the instructions on https://github.com/sebo-hri-lab/sebo-hri-lab.github.io to add your photo, name, and an optional web link to the People page.


For Those Getting Started on Programming Projects

If you've joined a lab project that involves robot programming, you'll want to:

  • Get invited to our Github organization https://github.com/SeboLab (you can ask one of your project team members or Sarah to add you to the Github organization)
  • Get familiar with ROS (the Robot Operating System), here are some recommended resources:
    • The beginner tutorials on ROS.org
    • The ROS Resources page on Sarah's Introduction to Robotics course site


For Those Getting Involved with Human Subjects Research Projects

Research projects involving human subjects data requires those involved to 1) have completed human subjects protection training and 2) have been added to the research project's IRB (internal review board) protocol.

If you have not yet completed human subjects protection training, you can find out how to complete that training on this website: https://sbsirb.uchicago.edu/training/. Once you complete your training, please email your certificate of completion to the project lead and they can add you to the IRB protocol.

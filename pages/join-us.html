PhD Students

We are always looking for highly motivated students interested in human-robot interaction, human-computer interaction, and/or robotics. If you are a prospective graduate student, please apply to the UChicago CS PhD program and mention your interest in working with PI Sarah Sebo in your application. The application submission deadline is typically around December 15. You can find more out online about the PhD program in CS at UChicago and more about the UChicago HRI lab on this site.

PI Sarah Sebo is not available this cycle for any video calls with student applicants in advance of application submission. After reviewing your application in December/January, Sarah will reach out to you via email if she would like to schedule a video call to learn more about your background and research interests.

Current UChicago Undergraduates and Masters Students

We encourage and welcome the involvement of UChicago undergraduate and Masters students in the HRI lab. UChicago undergrad and Masters students typically work collaboratively with other PhD students, undergraduates, and high school students on ongoing research projects that explore novel human-robot interactions. Work on human-robot interaction projects can include: reading academic papers to understand prior work and inform project work, experiment design, robot and system programming (typically done using Python), running a human subjects study, analyzing data, and paper writing. Students usually begin by joining an ongoing project and, if they choose, can later lead their own projects once they've gained more experience. Read below to find out more about the two primary ways you can get involved.

Summer Research Internship

  • Description: Summer interns work on one human-robot interaction research project in collaboration with PhD student(s), postdocs, and fellow summer interns. Students spend a majority of their time working collaboratively with their project team and also engage in several other regular meetings: a weekly lab-wide progress update meeting, a weekly project meeting with PI Sarah Sebo, and a weekly lab-wide reading group.
  • Timing & Location: Summer internships are full-time (37.5 hours/week) and typically run for 10 weeks from mid-June through the end of August. HRI lab students work in-person in John Crerar Library.
  • How to Apply: To apply for this summer internship position, submit your application via Handshake. The application deadline this year is February 20, 2026.
  • Questions: If you have any questions about the summer internship, please feel free to email PI Sarah Sebo (sarahsebo@uchicago.edu).

Research Assistant Position during the Academic Year

  • Description: Student research assistants typically join an ongoing research project team led by a current PhD student, postdoc, or undergraduate in the lab. Beyond contributing to a specific research project, students also participate in our weekly HRI lab meeting.
  • Time Commitment: We expect students working in the lab during the school year to devote about 10 hours/week on their research project.
  • How to Apply: To apply for a research assistant position in the HRI lab, please fill out this application form. Upon review of the submitted applications, a member of the lab will reach out with a request to interview applicants whose skills and interests align with current projects seeking new members.
  • Questions: If you have any questions about getting involved with the HRI lab or would be interested in a list of current projects in the lab, please email PI Sarah Sebo (sarahsebo@uchicago.edu).

High School Students

If you are a current high school student and are interested in getting involved with the HRI lab, apply to one of the following programs:

  • For all high school students in the Chicago area:
    • The Data Science Institute Summer Lab program
  • If you are a high school student within the Chicago public school system:
    • The Office of Special Programs College Prep with UChicago, and specifically the UChicago STEM initiative
    • The Collegiate Scholars Program with UChicago
  • If you are a high school student at the University of Chicago Laboratory Schools:
    • Connect with Jeremy Schwartz (jschwartz@ucls.uchicago.edu) and the CS SummerLink program.

Unfortunately, we are currently not supporting any high school student involvement in the HRI lab for students who live outside the Chicago area or do not qualify for the programs listed above.
